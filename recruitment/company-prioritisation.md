# Company Prioritisation

This is the working decision guide for ranking target companies. It is distilled from `inbox/chatgpt/2026-05-08-002-company-prioritisation.md` and should be refreshed before major application, relocation, compensation, or interview decisions.

## Decision Criteria

Prioritise roles that can credibly move Eddie from a strong Principal Product Designer / AI Design Lead baseline into a Staff / Principal-level environment with higher career signal, stronger AI relevance, better product ambition, and meaningful scope.

Use these criteria when ranking a company or role:

- AI is core to the product, not decorative or vague.
- The role has Staff / Principal scope: ambiguous product problems, strategic influence, systems thinking, and cross-functional leadership.
- The company has strong career signal through frontier AI, product quality, category momentum, or elite product/design standards.
- The work fits portfolio angles that can be verified against `recruitment/portfolio-positioning.md`: AI design leadership, marketplace/search systems, cross-platform product design, technical UX, product systems, enterprise AI, growth/product strategy, or high-craft product design.
- The opportunity can beat the AutoScout24 baseline on scope, trajectory, compensation, AI relevance, career signal, or quality of work.
- Relocation, visa friction, or life disruption is justified only when upside is meaningfully stronger.
- The role avoids under-leveling, narrow execution, vague strategy, or title inflation without real decision authority.

## Cross-Company Rules

- Treat AI-native product-shaping roles as the top of the search.
- Treat marketplace/search companies as relevant only when senior scope is clearly stronger than the current AutoScout24 baseline.
- Treat local Berlin roles as attractive only if they beat the baseline on more than convenience.
- Treat US frontier AI roles as relocation-worthy only when role scope, compensation, equity, and career signal are exceptional.
- Treat rejections as useful signal, not permanent closure; revisit only when a materially better-fit role appears.
- Keep compensation and equity details in `private/` when they become specific.

## Refresh Checklist

Before making a decision from this prioritisation, verify:

- Current open roles and whether the specific target role still exists.
- Location, hybrid, relocation, visa, and work authorization requirements.
- Compensation bands, equity potential, and total upside.
- Funding, growth, product traction, and company trajectory.
- Design team size, leadership, reporting line, and maturity.
- Whether the role is truly Staff / Principal in scope, not just in title.
- Whether the work is product-shaping or narrowly executional.
- Hiring manager expectations and portfolio review format.
- Whether AI is core to the product, adjacent, or mostly marketing.
