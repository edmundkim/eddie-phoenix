## Prioritisation criteria

The job search has been prioritised around roles that can credibly move Eddie from a strong Principal Product Designer / AI Design Lead baseline into a Staff / Principal-level environment with higher career signal, stronger AI relevance, better product ambition, and meaningful scope.

Primary criteria:

- **AI relevance:** Higher priority for companies where AI is core to the product, not a side feature or vague innovation layer.
- **Staff / Principal scope:** Higher priority when the role appears to involve product-shaping, ambiguous problem spaces, strategic influence, systems thinking, and cross-functional leadership.
- **Career signal:** Higher priority for companies with strong market recognition, category-defining products, elite product/design standards, or frontier AI credibility.
- **Product/design quality:** Higher priority for companies known or believed to have high craft expectations, strong product taste, and mature product/design collaboration.
- **Company trajectory:** Higher priority for companies with strong growth, category momentum, funding strength, or product-market pull. This must be refreshed before decisions.
- **Portfolio fit:** Higher priority when Eddie can credibly use high-level portfolio tags such as AI design leadership, marketplace/search systems, cross-platform product design, technical UX, product systems, growth/product strategy, enterprise AI, or high-craft product design.
- **Compensation/equity upside:** Higher priority when the upside could justify leaving a stable current role, especially for US or frontier AI companies. Exact compensation assumptions should be refreshed and kept private where needed.
- **Location or relocation burden:** Lower priority when the role requires relocation to a less attractive city, creates visa friction, or imposes major life disruption without sufficient upside.
- **Design maturity:** Higher priority when design appears to have real influence. Lower priority when the role risks being execution-heavy, founder-driven without design leverage, or unclear in reporting line.
- **Risk of being under-leveled:** Lower priority when the title or scope may map closer to Senior Product Designer despite Staff/Principal expectations.
- **Risk of role being too narrow:** Lower priority when the role seems limited to documentation, UI polish, marketing surfaces, support workflows, or implementation detail without strategic product ownership.

The prioritisation is judgment-based and should not be treated as final career advice without refreshing current company facts and role details.

## Cross-company patterns

- AI-native roles are highest priority when the role is genuinely product-shaping, not just UI execution or AI feature decoration.
- The strongest opportunities combine AI product ambiguity, senior IC influence, product systems, and credible design craft expectations.
- European AI companies are especially attractive when they reduce relocation burden while still offering strong career signal and upside.
- US frontier AI roles may justify major relocation only when compensation, equity, role scope, and career signal are clearly exceptional.
- Marketplace/search companies remain relevant because they map well to Eddie's existing strengths, but they need strong senior scope to justify leaving AutoScout24.
- Local Berlin companies need to beat the current AutoScout24 baseline on at least one of: scope, trajectory, compensation, AI relevance, or career signal. Ideally they beat it on several.
- A strong title alone is not enough. The role must avoid under-leveling, narrow execution, and vague "strategy" without real decision authority.
- High-craft product companies are attractive, but Eddie's portfolio must show sharper interface judgment and product taste, not only senior strategy.
- Developer tooling and technical UX roles are promising, but require the portfolio narrative to make technical product judgment more explicit.
- Enterprise AI roles can work well if they involve complex workflows, trust, automation, product systems, and cross-functional influence.
- Relocation-heavy roles should be compared against actual upside, not prestige alone.
- Rejections should not automatically remove a company forever, but they should lower near-term priority unless a better-fit role appears.
