## Target Hiring Narrative

Eddie is a Principal Product Designer and AI Design Lead who works across marketplace/search complexity, cross-platform product systems, and practical AI design leadership.

The strongest recruitment signal is not generic AI enthusiasm. It is the ability to translate ambiguous product and technology shifts into usable product systems, design standards, prototypes, workflow change, and public-safe customer experience work.

Use this through-line when tailoring applications:

- Complex consumer decisions: search, filters, listings, comparison, trust, and intent expression.
- Product-system thinking: patterns that work across platforms and teams, not isolated screens.
- Practical AI leadership: guiding teams on trust, control, disclosure, standards, prototyping, and team enablement.
- Senior IC judgment: framing ambiguous problems, making tradeoffs, aligning partners, and staying close to craft.

Avoid positioning Eddie as:

- A generalist UI designer with AI interest.
- An AI tooling hobbyist.
- A design manager in disguise.
- A portfolio that relies on broad AI claims without product evidence.

## Portfolio Review Flow

Use `recruitment/messaging.md` for full interview language. For case selection, keep this order in mind:

1. Open with the through-line: marketplace/search systems, cross-platform product work, practical AI leadership, and senior IC judgment.
2. Lead with the strongest case for the role, not the newest case by default.
3. Pair one shipped/product-impact case with one seniority/AI/systems case when the interview allows two examples.
4. Use supporting legacy work only when it clarifies a specific role fit.
5. Close by connecting the cases to the company's current product problems.

Good case pairings:

- AI company: Strategic AI plus New Search or Declutter.
- Marketplace/search company: New Search plus Declutter.
- High-craft product company: Declutter plus New Search.
- Automotive-adjacent company: New Search plus Strategic AI, with Daimler or Smart only if useful.
- Enterprise/workflow AI company: Strategic AI plus New Search as structured decision support.

## Claim-Safety Rules

- Metrics must come from `portfolio-site/RESUME.md` or case-study source files.
- Keep platform-specific evidence platform-specific.
- Distinguish shipped work, tested prototypes, internal standards, early concepts, and speculative ideas.
- Separate direct product-design ownership from AI direction. Safe default: Eddie designs search, discovery, listing, and buyer experiences hands-on while guiding teams on AI interaction patterns, prototypes, and frameworks.
- Use generalized evidence language when internal details are not public-safe.
- Keep unreleased AI concepts, internal AI roadmaps, sensitive prototypes, proprietary workflows, stakeholder material, and dashboards out of versioned recruitment files.
- Do not imply AutoScout24 endorsement of portfolio claims.
- Do not copy internal language into public case studies.
- Keep target-company opinions in recruitment files, not public portfolio pages.
