## Reusable Message Blocks

Use this section for reusable language, not final copy. Final outreach should still be tailored to the company, role, and actual contact.

### Positioning

- Principal Product Designer focused on marketplace search, AI design leadership, systems thinking, and shipped product impact.
- Staff / Principal IC target: broad scope, product strategy, systems thinking, cross-functional leadership, and hands-on design craft.
- Strongest reusable portfolio tags: AI design leadership, marketplace/search systems, cross-platform product design, product systems, technical UX, and high-craft product design.
- Use enterprise AI, developer tooling, and AI-native prototyping language only when the role framing is about standards, prototyping, internal enablement, or complex technical workflows.
- Separate hands-on product design from AI direction. Safe default: direct ownership of search, discovery, listing, and buyer experiences; AI work framed as patterns, frameworks, prototyping support, and team enablement unless a specific case proves shipped AI product ownership.

### Evidence To Reuse Carefully

- AutoScout24 search and listing-card impact should be checked against `portfolio-site/RESUME.md` and relevant case studies before reuse.
- AI-assisted, conversational, and customer-service AI claims should be checked against `recruitment/portfolio-positioning.md`; do not imply direct hands-on shipping unless the evidence supports it.
- Portfolio case selection and company-specific angles should be checked against `recruitment/portfolio-positioning.md` before portfolio reviews.
- CryptoXpress claims should stay factual and avoid unsupported detail unless source material is provided.

### Keep Out Of Versioned Materials

- Current salary, compensation floor, negotiation strategy, and private relocation constraints.
- Detailed visa, Blue Card, permanent residency, or immigration notes.
- Private emotional reflections, rejection processing, urgency, or personal-risk notes.
- Recruiter names, hiring-team names, and company-specific follow-up history unless they belong in a private or company-specific note.
- Confidential AutoScout24 metrics, dashboards, internal documents, stakeholder material, unreleased strategy, or roadmap details.
